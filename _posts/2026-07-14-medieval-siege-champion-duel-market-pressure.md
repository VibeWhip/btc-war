# Medieval Siege vs Champion Duel: Two Views of Crypto Market Pressure

Market pressure can be shown as a crowd or as a contest between two representatives. Medieval Siege and Champion Duel explore those two extremes. One scene uses large armies, fortifications, cavalry and artillery. The other compresses the same BUY-versus-SELL idea into a continuous one-on-one fight.

Neither scene predicts the next market move. Both visualize current public inputs from the selected Binance Spot market: price, aggregate trades, and visible order-book depth. Their purpose is to make changes in activity easier to perceive while preserving the uncertainty of the underlying market.

## Medieval Siege: pressure at army scale

![Medieval Siege with opposing fortresses, infantry, archers, cavalry and catapults]({{ '/assets/screenshots/siege.webp' | relative_url }})

Medieval Siege supports larger formations than the other ground scenes. Each side begins with a substantial army, and live order intensity can increase the population within a bounded range. Green and red uniforms keep the two factions legible when the center becomes crowded.

The headquarters sit behind longer four-sided walls with open front gates. New soldiers use those openings instead of appearing through solid geometry. Infantry advances for contact combat, cavalry moves faster, and archers can occupy elevated wall positions. High-ground archers gain a clearer firing lane but become easier targets for opposing ranged units.

Walls have their own health. Repeated bombardment can produce visible cracks, while delayed recovery prevents damage from becoming a permanent broken state. Artillery is reserved for large market events rather than firing continuously. Catapults launch visible burning boulders from real platforms, raise dust, and create a controlled impact shake when the volley lands.

The central flag shows whether BUY or SELL pressure leads, and a soldier from the dominant faction remains beside it. Because units can use multiple approach lanes, the fight develops across a broad front instead of trapping both armies inside their own bases.

## Champion Duel: pressure as one continuous fight

![Champion Duel with green BUY and red SELL fighters, health bars, combos and finishers]({{ '/assets/screenshots/duel.webp' | relative_url }})

Champion Duel removes the army and keeps only two representatives. The BUY fighter wears green and the SELL fighter wears red. Both remain active instead of entering a scheduled resting phase, and the combat space is wider than a static face-to-face exchange.

Each champion owns twenty named skills distributed across attacks, blocks, movement, combos, and finishers. The animation system varies those skill families so the duel does not repeat one identical strike. Health bars follow the fighters, keeping damage readable while they move.

When a health bar reaches zero, damage pauses for a three-second recovery sequence. The defeated champion's health refills before combat resumes. This creates a clear round boundary without adding chairs, a separate resting state, or a long break in the action.

The audio language is different from the fantasy and space scenes. Layered medieval steel impacts, heavier finishers, and controlled camera shake reinforce close-range contact. The shake remains intentionally smaller than earlier versions so feedback does not obscure the fighters.

## Why both views are useful

Army scale is good at showing concentration, reinforcement, flanking, and pressure across space. A duel is good at showing momentum, damage, recovery, and alternating control between two readable actors.

The scenes also expose different risks in a visual metaphor. A large army can look more certain than the underlying data really is. A champion winning one exchange can resemble a directional signal. BTC War avoids that interpretation by keeping the live data visible and by stating the boundary directly: current pressure is not a guaranteed future outcome.
